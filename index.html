About

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Harvard University, working at the intersection of bioelectronics, spatial omics, and artificial intelligence. My research focuses on developing innovative technologies that seamlessly integrate electronics with biological systems to understand complex tissue dynamics and advance precision medicine.

My work combines cutting-edge bioelectronic devices with spatial transcriptomics and AI-driven analysis to create multimodal platforms for studying organ development, disease progression, and therapeutic interventions. I am particularly interested in developing "tissue-like" electronics that can chronically interface with biological systems while maintaining their natural properties.

Current Research Focus

My current research develops next-generation bioelectronic interfaces that can seamlessly integrate with developing tissues. I combine this with spatial transcriptomics to create comprehensive atlases of tissue development and function. Using AI agents, I analyze complex multimodal biological data to identify key developmental pathways and disease mechanisms.
